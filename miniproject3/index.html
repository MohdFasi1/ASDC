biryani Recipe

Biryani is one of the most amazing royal delicacies introduced to Indians by the Persians. Since then it has been much popular and is considered to be a luxurious treat to enjoy on special occasions. Making a chicken biryani in authentic dum style does take some time and needs little expertise.

Ingredients
  • This recipe needs half kg chicken (1.1 lbs.). Make few slits on all the chicken pieces and add to a large bowl. Then add
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional)
  • 3 tablespoons plain yogurt (Indian curd)
  • 1¼ tablespoons ginger garlic paste
  • ½ to 1 tablespoon garam masala (or biriyani masala)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ½ to 1 teaspoon red chili powder (reduce for kids)
Instructions to prepare

Marinating the Chicken:
In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, salt, and vegetable oil.
Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated.
Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.

Cooking the Rice:
Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
In a large pot, bring 4-5 cups of water to a boil.
Add the soaked rice, green cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, bay leaf, and salt to the boiling water.
Cook the rice until it is about 70-80% cooked (it should still have a bite to it). Drain the rice and set it aside.

Frying the Onions:
Heat some oil in a pan and add the thinly sliced onions.
Fry the onions until they are brown and crispy. Remove half of the fried onions and set them aside for garnishing.

Assembling the Biryani:
In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or biryani pot, add a layer of marinated chicken at the bottom.
Top the chicken with a layer of cooked rice.
Sprinkle some chopped mint leaves, chopped cilantro, chopped green chilies, and the fried onions that you set aside earlier.
Repeat the layers until all the chicken and rice are used up, with the final layer being rice and garnishes.
Drizzle ghee over the top layer.

Cooking the Biryani:
Place the pot on low heat and cover it with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with dough to trap the steam.
Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the rice is fully cooked and aromatic.
You can also place a tava (griddle) under the pot to ensure even cooking and to prevent the bottom from burning.
Once done, remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before opening the lid.

Garnishing:
Before serving, garnish the biryani with saffron-soaked milk and the remaining fried onions.
Serve your homemade chicken biryani with raita (yogurt sauce) or a side salad. Enjoy your delicious homemade biryani!

Aloo Paratha Recipe

If you’re a fan of bread and potatoes, you’re going to love this Punjabi Aloo Paratha or Aloo ka Paratha. Sometimes spelled Alu Paratha, this potato stuffed flatbread is a popular Indian breakfast that combines two of my favorite starches in a single delectable package. Crisp on the outside and tender on the inside, this humble meal is as comforting as a hug. What’s not to love?

Ingredients
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • salt
  • water
  • potatoes
  • ghee or oil
  • Cumin seeds
  • Onions
  • Green chillies
  • Red chilli powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Amchur powder
Instructions to prepare

1. Prepare the Dough:
a. In a mixing bowl, add the whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt.
b. Gradually add water and knead the dough until it's smooth and soft.
c. Add a tablespoon of oil or ghee and knead the dough again until it's well incorporated.
d. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.
2. Prepare the Potato Filling:
a. In a mixing bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, finely chopped onion (if using), green chilies, grated ginger, cumin seeds, garam masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, salt, and chopped coriander leaves (if using).
b. Mix everything well to make a uniform filling.
3. Make Aloo Parathas:
a. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls, slightly smaller than a golf ball.
b. Take one dough ball, flatten it slightly, and dip it into dry wheat flour.
c. Roll out the dough ball into a small circle, about 4-5 inches in diameter.
d. Place a portion of the potato filling (about 2-3 tablespoons) in the center of the rolled-out dough.
e. Carefully fold the edges of the dough over the filling, sealing it completely to form a stuffed ball.
f. Gently flatten the stuffed ball with your hands.
4. Roll Out Aloo Parathas:
a. Dip the stuffed ball into dry wheat flour again.
b. Roll out the stuffed ball into a paratha, about 7-8 inches in diameter. Be gentle while rolling to prevent the filling from coming out.
c. You can make the paratha as thin or thick as you prefer.
5. Cook Aloo Parathas:
a. Heat a griddle or a flat pan over medium-high heat.
b. Once the pan is hot, place the rolled-out paratha on it.
c. Cook for a minute or two until you see bubbles forming on the surface.
d. Flip the paratha and spread a little ghee or oil on the cooked side.
e. Flip again and spread ghee or oil on the other side. Press gently with a spatula.
f. Cook until both sides are golden brown and crispy.
g. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.
6. Serve: Serve hot Aloo Parathas with yogurt, pickles, or any chutney of your choice.
Enjoy your homemade Aloo Parathas!

Gulab Jamun Recipe

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian sweet, made from milk solids and soaked in sugar syrup. It's a delightful dessert that's perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • A pinch of cardamom powder
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying
Instructions:

1. In a mixing bowl, combine the milk powder, all-purpose flour, ghee, milk, and baking soda. Knead the mixture into a smooth and soft dough. Add a little more milk if needed.
2. Divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and shape them into round balls, ensuring there are no cracks.
3. Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan over medium-low heat. Fry the dough balls in batches until they turn golden brown, making sure to maintain a low heat to cook them evenly without burning.
4. Remove the fried Gulab Jamuns from the oil and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
5. In a separate saucepan, prepare the sugar syrup by dissolving 2 cups of sugar in 1 cup of water. Add a pinch of cardamom powder for flavor.
6. Simmer the sugar syrup for about 5-7 minutes until it slightly thickens. It should have a sticky consistency.
7. Remove the sugar syrup from heat and let it cool for a few minutes.
8. Carefully place the fried Gulab Jamuns into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours so they can absorb the syrup and become soft and sweet.
9. Serve Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired. Enjoy this delectable Indian dessert!

Jalebi Recipe

Jalebi is a popular Indian sweet known for its deep-fried, coiled shape and sweet, syrupy taste. It's a delightful treat often enjoyed during festivals and celebrations.

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Oil for deep frying
Instructions:

1. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, gram flour, yogurt, baking soda, and ghee. Mix well to form a smooth batter. If desired, add saffron strands for color and flavor.
2. Cover the batter and let it ferment for about 6-8 hours or overnight. This will allow it to develop a slightly tangy flavor.
3. In a separate saucepan, prepare the sugar syrup by dissolving sugar in water. Add cardamom powder and simmer the syrup until it reaches a one-string consistency. Keep it warm.
4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. You can test if the oil is ready by dropping a small amount of batter into it; if it sizzles and rises to the surface, the oil is hot enough.
5. Pour the fermented batter into a squeeze bottle or a plastic bag with a small hole in one corner to create a makeshift piping bag.
6. Squeeze the batter into the hot oil in a spiral or pretzel shape, working from the center outwards. Fry until the jalebis turn golden brown and crisp.
7. Remove the fried jalebis from the oil and immediately dip them into the warm sugar syrup. Ensure they are well-coated in the syrup.
8. Allow the jalebis to soak in the syrup for a few seconds, then remove them and place them on a serving plate. Repeat the process with the remaining batter.
9. Serve Jalebi warm or at room temperature. They are best enjoyed fresh and can be garnished with chopped nuts if desired. Enjoy this sweet, syrupy delight!

Grilled Chicken Salad

A healthy and delicious salad that combines the goodness of grilled chicken with fresh vegetables and a flavorful dressing.

Ingredients:
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups mixed greens (lettuce, spinach, arugula, etc.)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, sliced
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup black olives, pitted and sliced
Instructions:

1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Season the chicken breasts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
2. Grill the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) and the chicken is no longer pink in the center. Remove from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into strips.
3. In a large salad bowl, combine the mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, feta cheese, and black olives.
4. Add the grilled chicken strips on top of the salad.
5. In a small bowl, prepare your favorite salad dressing (such as balsamic vinaigrette, ranch, or honey mustard).
6. Drizzle the dressing over the salad or serve it on the side.
7. Toss the salad gently to combine all the ingredients.
8. Serve the Grilled Chicken Salad immediately, garnished with additional feta cheese and a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper, if desired.
9. Enjoy your healthy and satisfying Grilled Chicken Salad!

Fruit and Yogurt Smoothie

A refreshing and nutritious smoothie packed with the goodness of fruits and yogurt, perfect for a quick and healthy breakfast or snack.

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup of mixed frozen berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional, for added sweetness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A handful of ice cubes (optional, for a colder smoothie)
Instructions:

1. Peel and slice the ripe banana.
2. In a blender, combine the mixed frozen berries, sliced banana, plain Greek yogurt, milk, honey or maple syrup (if using), vanilla extract, and ice cubes (if desired).
3. Blend all the ingredients until smooth and creamy. If the smoothie is too thick, you can add a little more milk to reach your desired consistency.
4. Taste the smoothie and adjust the sweetness, if needed, by adding more honey or maple syrup and blending again.
5. Once the smoothie is well-blended and reaches the desired consistency, pour it into a glass.
6. Optionally, garnish with a few fresh berries or a banana slice on top.
7. Serve your Fruit and Yogurt Smoothie immediately, and enjoy this nutritious and delicious drink!
